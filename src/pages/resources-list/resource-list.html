You should know...

If you or a friend has been a victim of sexual assault, it is important to understand the following:
  • It is not your fault, even if you were voluntarily using drugs or alcohol.
    (View the UmassD amnesty policy)
  • Your feelings are valid & important and it is your right to decide what to do.

Local Hospitals

  • You may seek medical care without reporting the crime to the police.
  • Hospital services may include the following:
    • Medical care, pregnancy testing, and drugs to prevent contracting a sexually transmitted disease to ensure victim saftey and comfort.
    • A Sexually Assault Nurse Examiner (SANE) is trained to collect forensic evidence, including clothing worn at the time of the assault. Showering or bathing may damage evidence and taking a change of clothing is recommend.

Local Police Stations

  • Can coordinate victim transportation, ensure victim safety, and take criminal reports.

On Campus Help

  • You have a right to confidentiality through our university's Title IX.
  • Legal advocacy services support survivors of sexual assault and domestic violence.

Local Counseling

  • You are entitled to emotional support.
  • Legal advocacy services support survivors of sexual assault and domestic violence.
